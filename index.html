About Me

Dr. Hamilton is a licensed clinical psychologist, having received her doctorate in Counseling Psychology from the University of Missouri-Columbia. She earned her B.A. in psychology with a minor in human development and family studies also from the University of Missouri-Columbia.

Dr. Hamilton has conducted individual, group, and couples’ counseling with adolescents and adults for several years in multiple settings. Dr. Hamilton is a generalist and works with people struggling with a variety of issues but also has specialized knowledge and expertise in the areas of trauma recovery, post traumatic stress disorder, relationship and family of origin issues, eating disorders and body image concerns, couples’ counseling, anxiety, women’s issues, and the use of mindfulness and yoga as therapeutic endeavors. As part of her integrative theoretical approach, Dr. Hamilton has specialized trauma training in Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing, EMDR. Dr. Hamilton has conducted extensive research in the area of societal influences on the development and prevention of eating and body image issues and has published articles and a book chapter on the prevention of eating disorders. She has also provided several guest lectures, outreach presentations, and invited talks on this subject matter as well as other topics, such as stress management utilizing mindfulness and yoga practices. Dr. Hamilton’s experiences have also included teaching yoga and leading mindfulness practice meditation groups.
